Our wedding party


Danielle's attendants
  • Susan Metallo, Maid Of Honor
    Having been friends since the second grade, it’s natural that we would have quite the stories to tell about one another. You’ve had the privilege of introducing me to the world of correct grammar, Shakespeare, the epicness of children’s literature, Monty Python, and poker. I’ve had the privilege of introducing you to Doctor Who (my *almost* sincere apologies to those who may not share this interest as much as we do), the Vlogbrothers, and Pirates of Penzance. Somehow we both simultaneously acquired all things Piratical as a hobby while managing not to seriously reenact the battles we read about with our brothers as the enemy… While it would be easy to say our friendship is built around tv shows and movies, Monk, Psych, Doctor Who, The Simpsons, My Fair Lady, Clue, and Young Frankenstein, are just a few that come to mind, it’s always been more than that. How often do you find 9 and 10 year-olds seriously discussing and trying to figure out what the deal is predestination and how it fits with free will, a conversation which resulted in an analogy we both use to this day? You have brought so much into my life through your passions, whether it be for food or Hamlet, and you always make me want to know more, if for no other reason than to be able to hold a conversation with you. I always look forward to our ridiculously long e-mails back and forth (one would think we’d figure out how to get those under control by now…) and when we talk it frequently seems as though we have both found some new obsession to share completely independent of one another. This is an incredible testament to the depth of our friendship, if we still used words such as Bosom Buddies you would be the definition. I love you.
  • Julie Kittel, Bridesmaid
    Julie, Julie, Julie. While we’ve known each other since the third grade, when I think of when we truly became friends my mind jumps to the end of middle school and really, mostly to our high school years. I can’t picture my life without music in it and I can’t picture band without you being there. Whether it was getting out of PE to go to band (what were our teachers thinking???) or practicing flute/clarinet duets during every free moment we had, you were always there. It was you I have to thank for my time working at Music & Arts which, while in and of itself was not the most positive experience, working with you, getting bagels from Whole Foods, and chauffeuring you around are some of my fondest memories from high school. There’s a certain bond that people share when they have spent a great deal of their time together counting, in increments of 4, 64 bars of rest; over, and over, and over again. But would I change a minute of it? Would I want road trips with you where the car actually worked the entire time? Would I want to start at VCU without you in every single one of my music classes? That would mean never skipping keyboarding to grab breakfast and good conversation, studying for theory together, or practicing completely different pieces, in the same room, at different tempos, in different keys. All of which are just a small sampling of the many things I cannot imagine my life without and all of them have you as the common thread. You showed me what it looked like to take music seriously at a time when I didn’t know how seriously I wanted to take it. And as music has become less of the string that holds us together we’ve discovered the friendship that we are left with is one not to be toyed with. I love you and thank you.
  • Laine Bryant, Bridesmaid
    Laine and I go all the way back to the second grade. A simpler time. One where I wore purple and pink, we chased boys around the playground, and participated in all things Girl Scouts related. Too quickly we hit middle school where we found ourselves replacing the bright colors with darker shades, we learned to carry on our friendship without being in the same classes through epic lunches and frequent movie fests. High school arrived and we found ourselves delving into movie fests with the kind of ferver and passion not typically found in a group 15-18 year-olds watching Disney movies mixed in with the PG13 or R rated ones we could convince our parents to let us watch. Pirate parties began in earnest, first as a celebration of the end of the school year and then because who wouldn’t want to have a pirate party with canoes and paddleboats on a pond? In typical us fashion a Disney rave during beach week was our method of showing the world we were grown-ups. College threw us for a loop but we quickly learned that e-mails and facebook messages combined with yearly trips to Busch Gardens and increasingly elaborate Pirate parties made it feel as though we had never gone away. Now, as we’re “adults” living closer than we have in years I find myself relishing the nights we go out for drinks and the depth of the conversations that we now have. I love you Laine and cannot wait to see where our friendship takes us next.
  • Shannon Robertson, Bridesmaid
    Unlike the other girls Shannon and I have not known each other pretty much our entire lives but from the depth of our friendship you would never know that was the case. We didn’t really get to know each other until, upon learning that we would be leading a small group together at VCU we each had the same reaction: I suppose I should get to know her better. As I think about where we’ve come since then the list is long. Whether it was leading small group together…twice, realizing we suck at actually planning for SG and are far more interested in asking about each other’s lives, living together, planning Super Secret Surprise Fantastic Days of Intrigue, driving to C’ville for dumplings, playing rousing games of Nertz, aiding Duncan in opening your eyes to all the movies you had missed out on, or our weekly phone calls, I cannot even begin to imagine my life without you in it. You have stood by me when I needed someone there and I am so excited to have you stand by me again. I love you.
  • Stephanie Bowman, Bridesmaid
    When I think on the friendship that has developed between us my mind never stays on one point for very long. We have that special friendship that feels like it’s been going on for forever as is want to happen with 5 and 6 year olds who have few tangible memories before that time. Hours have been spent, probably much to the dismay of our parents, bonding over being the oldest child while planning out ways to make ourselves heard. Joint family excursions of camping and backpacking were never far off in the distance. When you, Andrea, and Karisa would come up with one of your crazy play or video ideas you always backed me up to be the cameraman, music organizer, or some other role that did not involve being in front of the camera. There were sleepovers that continued well into our college years and the ever present butting heads when our stubbornness presented itself (as it so often did). We learned that we can go months without talking to each other and yet when we do we always pick up right where we left off. You have always been there for me and I know you always will be. The list never ends. I love you Stephanie and consider myself privileged to have you standing by my side in June.

  • Osman Surkatty, Groomsman
    Osman and I met in college when we both joined our major's team for a cyber defense competition. From those crazy, war-like couple of days feverishly hammering away at keyboards grew a friendship around a deep love of technology and understanding the people who interact with it.
