When it happened

Saturday, December 10, 2011

How we got engaged

I spent the week prior pretending to be really busy with work to make sure that we'd be hanging out all Saturday. On The Day, I ever so casually suggested that we take a walk through a park near her house that we'd been to many times before.

I spent the whole walk trying to find a perfect pause in the conversation near a scenic place on the walk with no one else around (frustratingly difficult to nail the timing on that one, 50 year old man in running shorts does not make for a romantic setting). I finally found the moment and started asking her if she had any preferences on types of engagement rings (knowing that I had gotten the info and bought the ring a month prior) or if she just wanted to be surprised. Danielle said that she just wanted to be surprised and unknowingly referred me to the same friend whose advice I used to get the ring currently burning a hole in my pocket. I said I'd look into it and try to surprise her while simultaneously pulling the ring out of my pocket, getting down on one knee, and asking "How about this one?". It was a beautiful day, she was completely surprised, and I actually remembered to say the words "Will you marry me?". All in all it worked out pretty well.

We walked on clouds back to her place, informed the families and headed out to what I promised would be a nice quiet dinner for the two of us. I took a back road to try and surprise her by taking her to the bar where we first met. She guessed the location a few blocks out and tried to console me, saying that it was OK since I already surprised her once. I thanked her for her graciousness while trying to suppress a huge smile because on the other side of the restaurant door was 20 of our closest friends, some of who had driven 3-5 hours to be there. She walked in and didn't register what was going on for a good 5 seconds before freaking out in the best way possible. 

It was one of the best days of our lives.
